Overview

What is Physical Stock Verification?

Physical Stock Verification — also known as a stock audit or physical inventory count — is an independent, on-site inspection and verification of a company's actual inventory against its recorded stock data. It involves physically counting, weighing, or measuring goods held at warehouses, factories, retail outlets, or distribution centres.

As a neutral third-party service provider, we conduct these audits entirely independently of your internal accounting or warehouse teams, ensuring the results are objective, accurate, and credible — whether required by a lending bank, a statutory authority, or your own management for decision-making.

Our stock audit reports are structured to highlight discrepancies, assess stock quality, identify slow-moving or obsolete inventory, and provide management with a clear picture of their actual asset position.

When is a Stock Audit Required?
  • Working capital loan renewals and bank financing arrangements
  • Statutory compliance under CARO 2020 and Companies Act provisions
  • Mergers, acquisitions, and due diligence exercises
  • Internal management control and loss prevention
  • Insurance claims involving inventory or warehouse assets
  • Year-end financial closing and balance sheet preparation
  • Franchise oversight and multi-location stock reconciliation
  • Supply chain risk assessment and vendor audits
